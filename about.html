About

My work lives in the space of pure abstraction, where painting is not about depicting the world, but about experiencing it from within.

My practice is driven by instinct as much as thought. Rooted in non-representational art, the subconscious, allowing the work to evolve organically, shaped by both intention and accident rather than following a fixed plan.

Until recently (2026), I worked without relying on photographs or external references, letting each piece emerge through intuition and an ongoing dialogue with the canvas. While I now explore ways to make the work more accessible to the eye, I feel my strongest work remains in pure abstraction.

Words have always been part of my expression. Present in my work since my earliest canvases in around 2011.

I come from a self-directed background, outside formal art education. This independence allows intuition to take precedence over convention, aligning my practice with abstract and process-driven traditions.

Ultimately, my paintings are not representations of something seen, but records of something felt.
